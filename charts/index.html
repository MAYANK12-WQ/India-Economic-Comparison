Chapter 1

The Starting Point

In 2004, India stood at a crossroads. A $700 billion economy, ranked 12th globally, with dreams of becoming an economic superpower.

When the UPA government took office in May 2004, India was already on an upward trajectory. The economy had begun liberalizing in 1991, and by 2004, the foundations were set for what would become one of the most remarkable growth stories in modern history.

The GDP stood at $720 billion. Foreign exchange reserves were at $141 billion. The world was watching as India emerged as a potential economic giant.

Chapter 3

Taming the Inflation Beast

One of the most significant achievements: bringing inflation under control.

High inflation erodes purchasing power and hurts the poor the most. During 2009-2013, India witnessed persistent double-digit inflation, with CPI touching 12.4% in 2009-10.

The introduction of inflation targeting by RBI brought inflation down to an average of 5.0% during 2014-2024.

The Story Continues...

Twenty years of data tell a story of remarkable transformation. India grew from a $720 billion economy to a $3.57 trillion powerhouse. 260 million people escaped poverty. The country rose from 12th to 5th largest economy.

Both the UPA and NDA eras contributed to this journey â€” each with distinct strengths. UPA saw higher headline growth during favorable global conditions. NDA achieved better inflation control and attracted more foreign investment, while navigating challenges like demonetization and COVID-19.
